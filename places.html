Mother Teresa's Kolkata - Places to visit in a day's trip

If you are planning to visit Kolkata, do checkout these places associated with Mother Teresa.

Name of the place Location Why should you visit?
The First House of the Missionary 14 Creek Lane in East Calcutta This was the place where she was enlightened of taking a bigger step towards serving the poor and open ‘Missionaries Of Charity’
St. Mary School (Loreto Bengali Medium School) 1 Convent Road, Entally. Because this is the school where she starting teaching in Calcutta. She took the 4 vows of “chastity, poverty, obedience and wholehearted free service to the poorest of poor”, during this period of time.
Nirmal Hriday (Immaculate Heart) – Motijheel School Behind the Loreto Entally Convent This is the start of the transformation of Mother Teresa. This is the exact spot in Calcutta where the Albanian-born nun first began her fabled mission to serve the poorest of the poor.
Mother Teresa’s first dispensary! No. 92, Acharya Jagadish Chandra Bose Rd, Maula Ali, Taltala, Kolkata, West Bengal 700014 This location is important as Mother Teresa opened her first free clinic out here primarily for the treatment of leprosy in the year 1948 after she left Loreto convent. The clinic is not the same as what it used to be but still quite significant as a part of her history and legacy in this city.
Nirmal Hriday 251, Kalighat road near Kalighat temple A sacred place where Mother and sisters searched for the ill and dying, giving them shelter and a place to die in peace.
Shishu Bhavan 78 A.J.C Bose Road This small unpainted bungalow was the first of several children’s homes established by the Missionaries of Charity. The infants were brought to the house, cleaned, fed, and given medical treatment.
The Mother House 54A. A.J.C. Bose Road Calcutta. It is the headquarters of the international religious congregation of the Missionaries of Charity. It has been home to Mother Teresa and her sisters from February 1953 to the present day. This place has the Mother’s room and the Mother’s tomb.
St Thomas Church Middleton Row, Park Street. The body of Mother Teresa was laid in this church for one week prior to her funeral, in September 1997.
